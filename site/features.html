T-Minus goes beyond sync. It understands your time, your relationships, and your commitments -- then gives you the intelligence to make better decisions about all three.

Foundation

Calendar Federation

Connect every calendar you use into a single canonical store. Events flow in via real-time webhooks, get classified and deduplicated, then project outward based on your policies.

⇄

Multi-Provider Sync

Google Calendar API v3 and Microsoft Graph integration. Each external account gets a dedicated Durable Object for serialized token refresh, cursor management, and per-account rate limiting.

⚡

Webhook-Driven Real-Time Updates

Push notifications arrive at the edge, get validated, and trigger incremental sync within seconds. Daily drift reconciliation catches anything webhooks miss -- because they will.

🔒

Envelope Encryption

OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM with per-account data encryption keys, wrapped by a master key. Token refresh is serialized through AccountDO to prevent races.

⚙

Policy-Based Projections

Define how events flow between accounts: busy overlays (default), detail mirroring, or custom projections. Loop prevention via extendedProperties markers ensures managed events are never re-ingested as origin.

How it works

01

Webhook fires from Google Calendar. Edge worker validates channel ID and resource state.

02

Sync message enqueued. Consumer pulls incremental delta via syncToken from AccountDO.

03

Events classified: origin, managed mirror, or foreign. Origins get canonical IDs (ULIDs).

04

Policy compiler computes projections. Stable hash comparison skips unchanged mirrors.

05

Write jobs enqueued with idempotency keys. Consumer executes Calendar API writes.

06

Append-only journal records every mutation with actor, change type, patch, and reason.

Intelligence

Scheduling Intelligence

Your calendar is not just a list of events. T-Minus computes cognitive load, quantifies context switch costs, and scores temporal risk -- so you can protect your time before it is gone.

≡

Cognitive Load Engine

Meeting density, fragmentation score, and aggregate daily/weekly metrics give you a quantified picture of how loaded your schedule actually is -- not just how full it looks.

⇆

Context Switch Cost Matrix

Events are categorized -- engineering, sales, admin, hiring, deep work -- and transition costs between categories are quantified. Back-to-back deep-work-to-sales is expensive. You should know that before it happens.

■

Deep Work Detection

Finds uninterrupted blocks, suggests optimizations, and measures the impact of proposed changes on your focus time before you commit.

⚠

Temporal Risk Scoring

Burnout risk from overwork patterns, travel overload from trip frequency, and strategic drift when time allocation diverges from stated intentions. Measured. Scored. Surfaced before it is too late.

Capabilities

What-If Simulation

Model the impact of adding a commitment before you say yes. See how a new weekly meeting affects your deep work blocks, context switch frequency, and cognitive load score.

Probabilistic Availability

Tentative events are weighted by historical cancellation rates. A "maybe" from someone who cancels 40% of meetings is very different from a "maybe" from someone who never does.

Constraint System

Buffer policies, working hours, no-meetings-after rules, and VIP overrides. Constraints compose and resolve to produce a schedule that respects your boundaries.

Awareness

Relationship Intelligence

Your calendar is a graph of human relationships. T-Minus tracks who you meet, how often, and whether those connections are thriving or fading -- then helps you do something about it.

⬡

Contact Graph

Categorized relationships -- colleague, friend, family, investor, client, board member -- with full interaction history. Know who matters and how much time you are actually spending with them.

⚖

Reputation Scoring

Reliability scores with recency decay. Reciprocity tracking with asymmetry detection. An interaction ledger tracking 7 outcome types with weighted scoring builds a picture you can trust.

⇣

Social Drift Detection

Daily computation, severity badges, and proactive alerts when relationships are fading. "You haven't met with Sarah in 3 months" is not a notification -- it is a signal.

⚑

Reconnection Suggestions

"You are in SF next week -- here are contacts you have not seen in 6 months." Geo-aware, context-aware, priority-weighted reconnection opportunities surfaced when they are actionable.

Governance

Commitment & Governance

Track where your time actually goes. Define commitments, monitor compliance, and produce verifiable proof -- for yourself, your clients, or your board.

☰

Time Allocation Tagging

Tag events with billable categories per client. Track time at the granularity that matters: by project, by client, by function. Automatic categorization from event metadata.

✔

Commitment Tracking

Rolling windows -- weekly and monthly -- with real-time compliance status. Compliant, under, or over threshold. Know before the period ends, not after.

⌘

Exportable Proof Documents

PDF and CSV exports with SHA-256 hash verification. Stored in R2 with 7-year retention. When someone asks "how much time did you spend on X?" you have a cryptographically verifiable answer.

⚒

Policy Merge

Org-level policy merge for enterprises: organization overrides compose with user preferences. VIP priority policies ensure the right people always get through.

Compliance flow

1 Define commitment: "20h/week for Client A"
2 Events auto-tagged by calendar, title pattern, or manual override
3 Rolling window tracks hours: real-time compliance status
4 Alerts fire when you are trending under or over threshold
5 End of period: export proof document (PDF/CSV)
6 SHA-256 digest computed, document stored in R2 (7-year retention)
